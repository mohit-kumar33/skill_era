# 7.7 Reputation Risk Management Plan

**Project Name:** Apex Arena (MVP)  
**Document Owner:** CEO / PR Lead  
**Version:** 1.0.0  

---

## 1. The Threat of Market Perception
In the RMG sector, trust is the only asset. If YouTube influencers or Reddit communities establish a consensus that "Apex Arena uses bots to steal money" or "Apex Arena blocks withdrawals when you win too much," the platform's LTV will instantly collapse to zero.

## 2. Mitigation via Radical Transparency

### 2.1 The "Provably Human" Guarantee
- **Action:** Publish a live ledger UI page showing anonymized, aggregated match data showing that 100% of collected entry fees flow directly back to human players minus the stated platform commission.
- **Commitment:** Explicit guarantee in the T&Cs: "Apex Arena will pay ₹10,00,000 to anyone who can definitively prove the platform injected an AI bot to participate in a real-money tournament."

### 2.2 Dealing with "The Algorithm is Rigged" Complaints
Losing players often externalize blame. "The matchmaking put me against a Grandmaster on purpose to make me lose."
- **Action:** Post-match, users can click an "Analyze Match" button that links directly to a Lichess/Chess.com analysis board snippet, proving their blunders via raw Stockfish evaluation.

## 3. Social Media Crisis Protocols

### 3.1 Severity Matrix
- **Level 1 (Routine Complaints):** "My withdrawal is taking 12 hours". *Response:* Automated Zendesk reply citing the 24-48 hour manual SLA.
- **Level 2 (Viral Cheating Allegation):** "Look at this video of my opponent moving automatically!" *Response:* Risk team publicly investigates the match ID, bans the opponent if guilty, refunds the victim, and posts the resolution publicly. Transparency wins fans.
- **Level 3 (Media/Govt Allegation):** "Apex Arena operating illegal gambling ring in Telangana." *Response:* Instant PR statement proving our hard IP and KYC blocks. Legal team issues Defamation Cease & Desist orders to publishers running unverified claims.
