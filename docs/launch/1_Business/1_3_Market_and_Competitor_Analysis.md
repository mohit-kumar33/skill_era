## 1. Market Overview (India RMG Sector)
The Indian Real-Money Gaming (RMG) market is one of the fastest-growing digital entertainment sectors globally, projected to cross $7.5 Billion by 2028. However, recent regulatory shifts (e.g., blanket 28% GST on deposits, stringent TDS rules under Section 194BA) have weeded out undercapitalized players and casual-luck games.

**The Opportunity:** 
A significant vacuum exists for purely deterministic, 100% skill-based games. While platforms like MPL and WinZO operate large catalogs (Ludo, Carrom, Fantasy), Chess remains relatively unmonetized in the RMG space despite massive organic popularity stemming from Indian Grandmasters.

## 3. Positioning Strategy
**Apex Arena "The Thinking Person's RMG"**
Instead of competing for the casual "time-killer" demographic playing Ludo for ₹5, Apex Arena targets the intellectual competitor willing to wager ₹50 to ₹1000 on their pure strategic superiority. 

**Key Selling Points (KSPs):**
1. **Zero Luck:** If you lose, it is strictly because you were outplayed. No dice, no cards, no RNG.
2. **Instant Withdrawals:** Frictionless Cashfree Payouts post-KYC/Cooldown.
3. **Pristine Compliance:** We do your taxes (30% TDS) automatically. You just play.

## 4. Threat Matrix
- **Threat 1: GST Burden:** The 28% deposit GST severely crimps unit economics. If competitors absorb it, we must too, increasing our CAC payback period.
- **Threat 2: Chess Engine Abuse (Cheating):** Users running Stockfish on a secondary device to win matches.
  - *Countermeasure:* Deep anti-cheat integration, move-time variance analysis, and forced browser-focus monitoring. (Covered extensively in SRD).
