# 1.1 Business Requirements Document (BRD)

**Project Name:** Apex Arena (MVP)  
**Document Owner:** Executive Management / Product Team  
**Version:** 1.0.0 (Production Launch Candidate)  
**Date:** March 2026  
**Jurisdiction:** Maharashtra, India  

---

## 1. Executive Summary
Apex Arena is a skill-based, real-money gaming (RMG) mobile and web application. The platform facilitates peer-to-peer (1v1) competitive chess tournaments where players pay an entry fee to compete, and the winner claims a predetermined prize pool. The company generates revenue by retaining a dynamic commission (platform fee) on the total tournament entry pool.

## 2. Business Objectives
- **Launch MVP:** Successfully launch a legally compliant, real-money chess platform capable of supporting 10,000 concurrent users (CCU).
- **Financial Viability:** Establish a profitable unit economics model through a 10-20% platform commission on every matched tournament.
- **Legal Resilience:** Operate strictly within the "Game of Skill" legal framework in India, enforcing hard geographic blocking on banned states (Telangana, Andhra Pradesh, Assam, Odisha, Nagaland, Sikkim).
- **Tax Compliance Automation:** Ensure zero liability for user tax evasion by automatically deducting and remitting 30% TDS under Section 194BA of the Indian Income Tax Act.

## 3. Product Scope
### 3.1 In Scope (MVP)
- **Core Gameplay:** 1v1 asynchronous and synchronous Chess tournaments.
- **Wallet System:** Segregated wallets (Deposit Balance, Winning Balance).
- **Payment Gateway Integration:** Cashfree Payments for deposits (UPI, Cards).
- **Payout Gateway Integration:** Cashfree Payouts for direct-to-bank withdrawals.
- **Compliance & KYC:** Automated Aadhaar and PAN verification via HyperVerge.
- **Tax Rules:** Automated 30% deduction on net winnings at the time of withdrawal.
- **Security:** 24-hour withdrawal cooldowns post-deposit, strict 1-account-per-KYC limitations, IP-based geo-blocking.

### 3.2 Out of Scope (MVP)
- Games with elements of chance (card games, dice games).
- Operations outside of the Republic of India.
- Bonus wallets, promotional credits, or affiliate referral cash (delayed to mitigate initial fraud risks).

## 4. Key Stakeholder Requirements
- **Investors/Board:** Require transparent double-entry ledger systems to ensure the company never subsidizes payouts from operational capital.
- **Legal Counsel:** Require foolproof technical implementation of state-level geo-blocking and strict AML (Anti-Money Laundering) checks to prevent platform seizure.
- **Payment Partners (Cashfree):** Require merchant compliance, clear refund policies, and sub-1% chargeback rates to maintain gateway access.

## 5. Risk & Constraints
### 5.1 Regulatory Risks
The Indian RMG sector is heavily scrutinized. Any ambiguity in the mechanical implementation of Chess could attract Public Interest Litigations (PILs).
- **Mitigation:** Rely explicitly on established Supreme Court precedents validating Chess as a game of skill. No RNG (Random Number Generator) mechanics influence the match outcome.

### 5.2 Financial Risks
Player withdrawals exceeding actual deposited/won funds due to race conditions or database deadlocks.
- **Mitigation:** Strict ACID-compliant database design (PostgreSQL), row-level locking on user wallet rows during financial mutations, and mandatory manual review of withdrawals during MVP phase.
